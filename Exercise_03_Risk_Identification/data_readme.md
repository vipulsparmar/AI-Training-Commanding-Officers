# Operational Scenario / Raw Data: Operational Risk Identification Drill (Sector: High Altitude Area / Induction)

**Classification: SECRET**
**TO: Commanding Officer, 9 JAK LI**
**FROM: GSO-3 (Ops/Air), HQ Mountain Brigade**
**DATE: 11 April 2026**
**SUBJECT: Concept of Operations (CONOPS) - Op "White Peak" (Battalion Induction)**

---

### **1. MISSION OVERVIEW**
The 9th Battalion, Jammu & Kashmir Light Infantry (9 JAK LI) is to be inducted into the High Altitude Area (HAA) sector of Dras/Kargil to replace 4 Mech Inf starting 15 April 2026. The induction involves moving 850 personnel, 40 vehicles, and 200 tons of winter equipment over the Zojila Pass.

### **2. CONVOY DETAILS & ROUTE**
*   **Route:** Srinagar -> Sonamarg -> Zojila Pass (11,575 ft) -> Dras.
*   **Vehicle Load:** 20x Ashok Leyland Stallions (Personnel), 10x 2.5 Tonners (Ammunition/Rations), 5x Fuel Tankers (POL), 5x Specialist Equipment Trucks (Climbing Gear/Oxygen).
*   **Timing:** Convoy to depart Sonamarg at 0200h to clear the Zojila bypass before the BRO (Border Roads Organization) halts traffic for snow clearing at 0800h.
*   **Speed:** Avg 10-15 km/h on the pass.

### **3. PERSONNEL & HEALTH STATUS**
*   **Acclimatization:** 70% of the Battalion has completed Stage 1 Acclimatization at Sonamarg. 30% (New joined/Leave returnees) have only had 2 days of Stage 1.
*   **Fitness:** Two JCOs and 12 Jawans reported symptoms of Acute Mountain Sickness (AMS) during a practice march yesterday. They are under observation at the medical room.
*   **Equipment:** Cold Weather Clothing (Special) issued. 15% of the parkas are reported as 'Grade B' (Used) with minor insulation wear.

### **4. TECHNICAL & LOGISTICS DATA**
*   **Vehicle Serviceability:** Fuel tankers reported minor leakages in sub-zero temperatures (-10°C). Mechanics using temporary sealants.
*   **POL:** High-speed Diesel (Winter Grade) is available, but the additive mix (Antifreeze) is only 80% of the required quantity.
*   **Rations:** 15 days of canned rations loaded. 3 days of fresh rations (poultry/veg) to be picked up at Dras.

### **5. INTELLIGENCE & THREAT ASSESSMENT**
*   **Enemy Posture:** Increased activities observed at adversary posts 'Pimple 1' and 'Gully'. SIGINT suggests deployment of specialized snipers in the sector.
*   **Infiltration:** Intelligence indicates a potential attempt by a small team to disrupt the Zojila MSR (Main Supply Route) using IEDs hidden in snow drifts.
*   **Local activity:** High movement of civilian nomadic traffic on the Dras side, complicating surveillance.

### **6. WEATHER & TERRAIN LOGS**
*   **BRO Report:** Zojila is currently 'Black Top' but black ice is frequent between KM 95 and KM 105. One landslide cleared yesterday, but the slope remains unstable.
*   **Weather Forecast:** A sudden drop in pressure at 1200h (Today) indicates a potential 'Whiteout' condition on the 15th (Induction day). Wind speeds likely to touch 70km/h. 
*   **Snow Accumulation:** Avalanche Risk Level: 3 (Moderate, but significant on South-facing slopes of Zojila).

### **7. RESOURCE CONSTRAINTS**
*   **Recovery:** Only 1x Heavy Recovery Vehicle (ARV) available for the entire convoy. If two trucks break down, the second must be abandoned or towed by a combat vehicle.
*   **Medical:** 2x Ambulances (Gypsy) in the convoy. No mobile ALS (Advanced Life Support) available.
*   **Communication:** HF (High Frequency) sets expected to have significant static during the Zojila crossing due to magnetic interference.
