MASTER PROMPT – GENERATE AI TRAINING EXERCISES FOR COMMANDING OFFICERS

Act as a military training designer preparing practical AI training exercises for commanding officers and senior staff officers.

Your task is to create 5 realistic operational exercises that demonstrate how AI can assist officers in staff work, intelligence analysis, and decision support.

Each exercise must follow this structure:

1. Exercise Title
   Give a clear and professional title (example: Intelligence Digest Builder, Situation Report Generator, Risk Identification Drill, Course of Action Generator, Decision Brief Builder).

2. Training Objective
   Explain in 2–3 sentences what skill the officers will learn through the exercise.

3. Operational Scenario / Raw Data (1–2 pages)
   Provide a realistic operational input document similar to what an operations room or intelligence cell would receive.

The raw data should include fragmented information such as:

* Patrol reports
* UAV surveillance observations
* Logistics updates
* Signals intelligence
* Weather information
* Local population activity
* Communication disruptions
* Terrain or infrastructure issues

Write this section as a 1–2 page operational note with headings and detailed narrative similar to military situation logs or intelligence updates.

4. Task for Officers
   Provide a clear task officers must perform using AI.

Example tasks:

* Convert the raw data into a 30-second operational briefing
* Create a structured SITREP
* Identify operational risks
* Suggest possible courses of action
* Produce a decision briefing for a brigade commander

5. AI Prompt to Use
   Provide the exact prompt officers should enter into AI to complete the task.

Example:
“Convert the following intelligence inputs into a structured SITREP with sections: Situation, Assessment, and Immediate Actions.”

6. Step-by-Step Exercise Instructions
   Provide a simple facilitation guide with steps such as:

Step 1 – Introduce the scenario to officers
Step 2 – Provide the raw operational inputs
Step 3 – Ask officers to run the AI prompt
Step 4 – Review the AI output
Step 5 – Discuss improvements and insights

7. Example AI Output
   Show a sample result that AI might produce so instructors understand the expected outcome.

8. Learning Outcome
   Explain what officers will learn from the exercise.

Focus on demonstrating how AI can:

* Structure information
* Summarise large inputs
* Identify risks
* Generate initial analysis
* Save time in staff work

9. Instructor Debrief
   Provide 2–3 discussion points the instructor can use to highlight the value and limitations of AI.

*   The tone should be professional and suitable for military training.
*   **Indian Army Focus:** Use standard Indian Army terminology (e.g., GOC, CO, Adjutant, JCO, NCO, Ghatak, RR, Bde, Bn, Coy). Situations should reflect Indian operational realities (LAC, LOC, CI/CT, HADR).
*   Each exercise should feel realistic and operationally relevant.
*   Raw data sections should be detailed enough (1–2 pages) to simulate real staff inputs with fragmented information, radio transcripts, and multi-agency logs.
*   Avoid fictional science-fiction scenarios; keep the context grounded in realistic military operational environments.
*   Ensure exercises demonstrate practical AI applications useful to commanding officers.

Generate 5 complete exercises using this format.
