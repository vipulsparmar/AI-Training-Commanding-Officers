---

### **1. THE CRISIS: GLOF INCIDENT**
A Glacial Lake Outburst Flood (GLOF) occurred at South Lhonak Lake at 0130h today. A massive volume of water is moving down the Teesta River basin. Multiple Army camps and civilian bridges are in the direct path.

### **2. CURRENT SITUATION (SENSOR & GROUND FEED)**

#### **A. Infrastructure Status (BRO & Army Engineers)**
*   **Bridge 1 (Chungthang):** **WASHED AWAY.** The 120-meter span is gone. 2x Truck convoys stranded on the North side.
*   **National Highway 10 (MSR):** Multiple landslides at KM 12, KM 18, and KM 45. Road link between Siliguri and Gangtok is **SEVERED.**
*   **Munsithang Army Camp:** 40% of the camp is submerged. Communication with the camp is patchy (only satellite phone working).
*   **Indo-Tibetan Border Police (ITBP) Post:** Reports that the landslide has created a 'Temporary Dam' on the Lachen Chu river. If this dam bursts, a second wave of water will hit the lower valley in 4 hours.

#### **B. Personnel/Life Status (Medical & Personnel)**
*   **Missing:** 22 Army personnel reported missing from a logistics park near the river bank. 
*   **Injured:** 14 personnel at the forward post have sustained 'Cold Water Immersion' injuries (Hypothermia).
*   **Civilian:** The town of Teesta Bazaar is being evacuated. Approx 2,000 civilians are currently on high ground but lack food and clean water.

#### **C. Aviation & Rescue Assets**
*   **Army Aviation (ALH Dhruv):** 4x Dhruv helicopters available at Bagdogra. However, the 'Cloud Ceiling' is currently below 500 feet. Flight operations impossible for the next 4 hours.
*   **NDRF (National Disaster Response Force):** 2 Teams (60 personnel) are at the Siliguri base with rubber boats. They cannot move forward because NH-10 is blocked. 
*   **IAF (Indian Air Force):** Mi-17 V5s on standby, but require a cleared Landing Zone (LZ) near Chungthang. The earlier LZ is now flooded.

### **3. MULTI-AGENCY COORDINATION LOGS (TRANSCRIPTS)**

*   *Civil Administration (DM Mangan):* "We are overwhelmed. The state disaster funds are tied up. We need the Army to take over the supply of rations to the 2,000 people in the hills. They have no medical aid."
*   *GOC 17 Mountain Div (Radio Intercept):* "The priority is to rescue our missing boys. Every hour counts. Forget the vehicles, we need people on the ground with satellite phones."
*   *BRO Chief Engineer:* "I can't clear the road till the rain stops. The hillside is still sliding. If I put my men there, it's a suicide mission. We need a 'Baileys Bridge' at Chungthang, but the components are at the base."
*   *Met Department (IMD):* "Red Alert for North Sikkim. Another 100mm of rain expected in the next 6 hours. The Teesta level will rise further."
*   *Liaison Officer to ITBP:* "Confirm the status of the 'Landslide Dam'. If it breaks, our camps at Dikchu are in the kill zone. We need a visual confirmation via UAV."

### **5. COMMANDER’S DILEMMA POINTS**
1.  **Prioritization:** Do we search for the 22 missing Army personnel or focus on the 2,000 civilians facing starvation?
2.  **Risk Management:** Do we authorize a 'Low-Visibility' ALH flight for urgent medical drop, risking the aircrew's life?
3.  **Communication:** How to manage the 'Information Vacuum' caused by NH-10 blockage and fiber-cut in North Sikkim?
4.  **Secondary Threat:** Monitoring and potentially 'Controlled Blasting' of the ITBP-reported landslide dam.
