# Exercise: Commander’s Decision Briefing Builder

### 1. Training Objective
Officers will use AI to condense a complex 20-page operational plan into a highly structured, 5-minute decision briefing for a General Officer.

### 2. Task for Officers
Use AI to create a **one-page Decision Briefing script**. The General needs to know exactly what the mission is, the risks, the required resources, and the "Go/No-Go" criteria.

### 3. AI Prompt to Use
> "ACT AS A MILITARY EXECUTIVE ASSISTANT. SUMMARIZE THE 'APEX HUNTER' MISSION INTO A 5-MINUTE DECISION BRIEFING FOR A BRIGADIER GENERAL. USE THESE SECTIONS: MISSION OBJECTIVE, KEY OPERATIONAL STEPS, CRITICAL RISKS, RESOURCE REQUIREMENTS, AND A RECOMMENDATION. THE TONE MUST BE PERSUASIVE BUT TRANSPARENT ABOUT RISKS."

### 4. Step-by-Step Exercise Instructions
*   **Step 1:** Provide the "Overview" of the plan (found in `data_readme.md`).
*   **Step 2:** Ask officers to generate the briefing.
*   **Step 3:** Have one officer "deliver" the briefing using only the AI output.
*   **Step 4:** Evaluate if the General would have enough information to make a decision.

### 5. Example AI Output
**DECISION BRIEF: OPERATION APEX HUNTER**

**1. MISSION OBJECTIVE**
Neutralize/Capture HVP "Kharon" at Mountain Compound within 48 hours to prevent target relocation.

**2. OPERATIONAL STEPS**
*   **Soft Opening:** Cyber-neutralization of SAM assets.
*   **Insertion:** Night-time air-assault (Task Force Black).
*   **Exfiltration:** Ground convoy through Sector West to the border.

**3. CRITICAL RISKS**
*   **Compromised Exfil:** Passing through the loyalist village may trigger civilian uprising or militia intervention.
*   **Diplomatic Friction:** Border crossing requires immediate authorization from 'Country X' state department.

**4. RESOURCE REQUIREMENTS**
*   1x MH-47 Chinook, 1x Cyber-warfare cell, $2.5M operational fund.

**5. RECOMMENDATION**
Proceed with Phase 1 and 2. Develop a secondary 'Air-Exfil' contingency to mitigate the risk of the loyalist village.

### 6. Learning Outcome
Officers will experience the power of AI in distillations—turning "wall of text" planning documents into clear, hierarchical decision points.

### 7. Instructor Debrief
*   **Point 1:** Did the AI identify the need for a contingency plan?
*   **Point 2:** How much more confident is the General when the brief is structured this way?
*   **Point 3:** Remind officers that AI doesn't "know" the General's personality—the briefer must still add the "personal touch."
