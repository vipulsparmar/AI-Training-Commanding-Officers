# Exercise: Commander’s Decision Briefing Builder (Indian Army Context)

### 1. Training Objective
Officers will use AI to condense a massive amount of fragmented data from multiple agencies (BRO, ITBP, Civil Admin, NDRF) into a 5-minute 'Go/No-Go' Decision Briefing for a General Officer. The focus is on "Triaging Requirements" during a humanitarian crisis.

### 2. Task for Indian Staff Officers
You are the **Brigadier (Ops)** or a **Staff Officer (GSO-1)** at the Integrated Command Center in Siliguri. You have the 'Rescue Himagiri' log (found in `data_readme.md`). The Area Commander (Major General) is arriving in 10 minutes and needs to decide on the **Aerial Rescue Launch** and the **Secondary Dam Threat**.

**Your Task:**
1.  Read the incoming logs from BRO, NDRF, and forward Army camps.
2.  Synthesize the conflicting priorities (Military search vs. Civilian relief).
3.  Produce a one-page **Decision Briefing Script** that identifies the 3 the most urgent command decisions.

### 4. AI Prompt to Use
> "ACT AS A SENIOR STAFF OFFICER (GSO-1 OPS) DURING A DISASTER RELIEF MISSION (HADR). BASED ON THE 'OP RESCUE HIMAGIRI' DATA, PREPARE A 5-MINUTE DECISION BRIEFING FOR THE GENERAL OFFICER COMMANDING (GOC). THE BRIEFING MUST INCLUDE: 1. THE CURRENT INFRASTRUCTURE CRIPPLE (BRIDGES/MSR), 2. THE STATUS OF MISSING PERSONNEL VS CIVILIAN EVACUEES, 3. THE IMMINENT THREAT OF THE SECONDARY LANDSLIDE DAM, 4. THE 'GO/NO-GO' RECOMMENDATION FOR HELI-RESCUE IN RED ALERT WEATHER. USE INDIAN TERMINOLOGY LIKE 'GOC', 'BRO', 'MSR', 'BAILEYS BRIDGE', 'GSO', 'ALH DHRUV'."

### 5. Step-by-Step Exercise Instructions
*   **Step 1:** Distribute the Sikkim GLOF logs (`data_readme.md`).
*   **Step 2:** Instruct officers to prioritize "The Landslide Dam" and "The Missing Personnel" in their mental map.
*   **Step 3:** Run the AI prompt to generate the briefing script.
*   **Step 4:** Role-play: One officer delivers the AI script to the 'GOC' (the instructor).
*   **Step 5:** Evaluate: Did the AI briefing give the General enough 'Assurance' to authorize a high-risk helicopter flight?

### 6. Example AI Output (Decision Briefing)
**GOC, BLUF:** Strategy for Op Rescue Himagiri is currently compromised by an 'Infrastructure Blackout' on NH-10 and a deteriorating weather window. We face a potential 'Secondary Catastrophe' if the Lachen Chu landslide dam bursts (ETA 4 hours).

**Key Decision Points:**
1.  **Heli-Rescue Authorization:** Requesting 'Go' for 2x ALH Dhruv on 'Low-Level Scuds' for urgent medical drop at Munsithang despite 500ft ceiling. **Recommendation: NO-GO** until 1400h due to safety; use civilian porter teams for foot-drop instead.
2.  **Infrastructure Priority:** BRO reports 12-hour delay. **Recommendation:** Authorize heli-lifting of Baileys Bridge components to Chungthang North side as soon as weather clears to restore tactical link.
3.  **Search vs. Relief:** 22 Personnel missing; 2,000 civilians starving. **Recommendation:** Divert NDRF teams to Civilian Relief; keep Munsithang organic troops focused on 'Search & Recovery' of missing personnel to maintain unit morale.

**Secondary Threat:** The Lachen Chu dam. **Action:** Deploy tactical UAV at first visible light to assess need for 'Controlled Blasting' by Army Engineers.

### 7. Learning Outcome
Officers experience the power of AI in "Operational Synthesis"—turning a 2-page list of disasters into a prioritized, actionable list of command decisions.

### 8. Instructor Debrief
*   **Point 1:** Did the AI notice the "Secondary Dam" threat? In GLOF scenarios, the second wave is often more deadly than the first.
*   **Point 2:** Discuss the recommendation on Heli-Rescue. Did the AI play it "too safe" or "too risky"?
*   **Point 3:** Emphasize that in HADR, the Adjutant becomes the "Liaison Hub" between civilian and military worlds—AI helps speak both languages.
