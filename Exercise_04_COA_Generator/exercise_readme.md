# Exercise: Course of Action (COA) Generator

### 1. Training Objective
Officers will learn to use AI to brainstorm multiple viable tactical options for a complex scenario, helping to break "linear thinking" during the Military Decision Making Process (MDMP).

### 2. Task for Officers
Use AI to generate **two distinct Courses of Action (COAs)**: one focusing on "Speed and Flanking," and one focusing on "Siege and Surgical Strike."

### 3. AI Prompt to Use
> "ACT AS A CHIEF OF OPERATIONS (G-3). BASED ON THE 'COASTAL SWEEP' SITUATION, DEVELOP TWO DISTINCT COURSES OF ACTION (COA). COA 1: 'SUDDEN STRIKE' (PRIORITIZING SPEED AND DECEPTION). COA 2: 'SURGICAL PRESSURE' (PRIORITIZING CIVILIAN SAFETY AND INFRASTRUCTURE PRESERVATION). FOR EACH COA, LIST: METHOD OF ENTRY, ROLE OF SPECIAL FORCES, AND RISK LEVEL."

### 4. Step-by-Step Exercise Instructions
*   **Step 1:** Explain the tactical problem and the constraints (civilian presence).
*   **Step 2:** Ask officers to generate the two COAs using AI.
*   **Step 3:** Evaluate the COAs for tactical feasibility and compliance with Rules of Engagement (ROE).
*   **Step 4:** Ask the AI to "wargame" COA 1 against a likely enemy reaction.

### 5. Example AI Output
**COA 1: 'SUDDEN STRIKE'**
*   **Method:** Amphibious landing from the seaward side at dawn to bypass the main road.
*   **Special Forces:** Infiltrate early to disable ZU-23-2 AA guns.
*   **Risk:** High. Potential for chaotic urban combat if deception fails.

**COA 2: 'SURGICAL PRESSURE'**
*   **Method:** Establish a cordon on the main road with tanks; use Naval Gunfire for "Precision Illumination" and psychological pressure.
*   **Special Forces:** High-altitude low-opening (HALO) drop or stealth boat entry for precision raids on specific warehouses.
*   **Risk:** Medium. Longer duration may lead to civilian food shortages.

### 6. Learning Outcome
Officers explore how AI can offer non-traditional tactical solutions (like amphibious entry) that their own branch biases might have overlooked.

### 7. Instructor Debrief
*   **Point 1:** Is COA 1 feasible with our current amphibious equipment?
*   **Point 2:** How can AI help evaluate the "Risk" more objectively?
*   **Point 3:** Use this to show that AI is a "brainstorming partner," not a "decision-maker."
