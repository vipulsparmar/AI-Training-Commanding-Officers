# Exercise: Course of Action (COA) Developer (Indian Army Context)

### 1. Training Objective
Officers will learn to use AI to generate multiple, distinct tactical Courses of Action (COAs) for a "Room/Dhok Intervention" in a CI-CT setting. The goal is to evaluate AI-generated options against Indian Army Doctrine on "Restraint" and "Minimum Force" while ensuring mission success.

### 2. Task for Indian Tactical Commanders
You are the **Officer Commanding (OC) of the Ghatak Platoon** or the **Adjutant of the RR Battalion**. You are faced with the 'Pampur Orchard' standoff (found in `data_readme.md`). The CO is looking for two distinct plans to present for the 0300h 'O-Group' (Orders Group).

**Your Task:**
1.  Analyze the dhok terrain and the 'Suitability' of the assets.
2.  Use AI to brainstorm two different strategies:
    *   **COA 1: 'The Stealth Strike'** (High speed, minimal noise, focus on capture).
    *   **COA 2: 'The Controlled Cordon'** (Safe distance, maximum psychological pressure, focus on neutralization).
3.  Evaluate the 'Civilian Risk' in both COAs.

### 3. AI Prompt to Use
> "ACT AS A BATTALION COMMANDER (CO) OF AN RR UNIT. BASED ON THE 'OP ORCHARD SWEEP' INTELLIGENCE, DEVELOP TWO DISTINCT COURSES OF ACTION. COA 1: 'SILENT INFILTRATION' (USING GHATAKS, CANAL APPROACH, FOCUS ON CAPTURE). COA 2: 'GRADUATED ESCALATION' (USING SNIPERS, PUBLIC ANNOUNCEMENTS, CORDON REINFORCEMENT). FOR EACH COA, PROVIDE: 1. PHASED EXECUTION PLAN, 2. UTILIZATION OF ASSETS (DOGS, EOD, UAV), 3. TACTICAL RISKS, AND 4. MITIGATION FOR CIVILIAN COLLATERAL. USE TERMS LIKE 'O-GROUP', 'GHATAK', 'DHOK', 'ROE', 'CASO'."

### 4. Step-by-Step Exercise Instructions
*   **Step 1:** Distribute the Pampur Orchard tactical map and logs (`data_readme.md`).
*   **Step 2:** Ask participants to identify the "Tactical Dilemma" (e.g., The basement cellar vs the suicide attacker threat).
*   **Step 3:** Run the AI prompt to generate the COAs.
*   **Step 4:** Critically analyze COA 1—Is the "Canal Approach" realistic for 6-foot deep water without specialized gear?
*   **Step 5:** Facilitate a discussion on ROE (Rules of Engagement). Did the AI suggest any action that violates the "Minimum Force" principle?

### 5. Example AI Output (Tactical COAs)
**COA 1: 'SILENT INFILTRATION' (The Ghatak Approach)**
*   **Method:** Breach from the North canal side using ropes/ladders. Use the poplar thicket for final stealth approach.
*   **Assets:** Dog squad used for early warning of perimeter traps. UAV in 'Silent Loiter' to monitor the cigarette-smoker.
*   **Ghatak Role:** Rapid entry via the South window (smoking point). Use of flash-bangs and corner-shot to clear the basement first.
*   **Risk:** High. If detected, Ghataks are in the open apple grove with low cover.

**COA 2: 'GRADUATED ESCALATION' (The Siege Approach)**
*   **Method:** Tighten the Cordon. Use the CO's 'Loudspeaker' to offer a chance of surrender (standard SOP). 
*   **Assets:** Sniper teams positioned in House No. 4 (after evacuating civilians). EOD ROV used to probe the entrance IEDs.
*   **Friendly Role:** Use heavy smoke to mask movement. If no surrender is offered by 0430h, deliver precision fire on the Pika gun positions.
*   **Risk:** Time. If the op lasts past 0530h, a large civilian mob will interfere.

### 6. Learning Outcome
Officers explore how AI can help "Wargame" a tactical problem from different angles—identifying that a 'Silent' approach might be compromised by something as simple as a 'cigarette-smoker' at a window.

### 7. Instructor Debrief
*   **Point 1:** Did the AI account for the 'fidayeen' threat in its breach plan?
*   **Point 2:** How did the AI handle the 'Civilian Evacuation' problem?
*   **Point 3:** Remind officers that AI-generated COAs are "Brainstorming Templates"—the final decision must account for the moral weight of civilian lives, which AI might treat as a simple "parameter."
